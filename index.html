The Amazon is the world’s largest tropical rainforest, covering over 5.5 million square kilometres. The Amazon is found in South America, spanning across Brazil, Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, Colombia, Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname and French Guiana. The Amazon receives between 59 and 118 inches of rain each year. The Amazon is home to at least 10% of the world’s known biodiversity, including endemic and endangered flora and fauna.



The Sahara is the largest hot desert in the world, covering much of North Africa. Saharan dunes can reach up to 180 meters in height. Dinosaur fossils have been found in the Sahara. The Sahara is mostly rock, not sand. It is the hottest region in the world during the day in summer, with temperatures above 120°F (49°C).



The Mariana Trench is the deepest oceanic trench on Earth, reaching a maximum depth of about 10,925 meters (35,843 feet) at its lowest point, known as the Challenger Deep.It is located in the western Pacific Ocean, about 200 kilometers (124 miles) east of the Mariana Islands.The trench is crescent-shaped, measuring about 2,550 kilometers (1,580 miles) in length and 69 kilometers (43 miles) in width.Despite its depth, parts of the trench are hot, and parts are cold.The Mariana Trench is nearly 30,000 feet deeper than the Grand Canyon in the United States.



Antarctica contains about 90% of the world's ice and 70% of the world's fresh water. It is the coldest place on Earth, with temperatures as low as -128°F. Antarctica is the 5th largest continent, double the size of Australia. It doesn't have a capital city, official language, or any country controlling it.
